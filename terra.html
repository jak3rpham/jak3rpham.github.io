Starting Point
When I joined, terra-plat.vn had ~170 organic clicks/day, no SEO foundation, no analytics infrastructure, and no systematic content strategy. The website existed but had a Semrush health score of 55 - with unresolved crawl errors, missing schema, poor Core Web Vitals, and no internal linking architecture.
Target audience

terra serves FDI companies and mid-to-large enterprises operating in Vietnam - primarily HR managers, CFOs, and business owners who need compliant payroll outsourcing, HR consulting, and accounting services.

The B2B buyer journey is long, research-heavy, and multilingual (Vietnamese + English for Japanese/European decision-makers). Content needs to earn trust before it earns leads.

My scope covered everything technical and creative: the website, SEO, analytics, ads monitoring, social assets, video production, and branding materials - while collaborating with the manager on strategy direction and the content strategist on editorial calendar.

04.5 - Technical Scope

Under the hood

SEO results don't happen without infrastructure. Three foundations were built in parallel during the first 90 days, then maintained continuously: schema architecture, Core Web Vitals optimisation, and internal linking topology.

01

7+ structured data types deployed

  • Service schema across payroll, HR, accounting, tax service pages — rich result eligibility for commercial queries
  • Article + BlogPosting for guides and explainers
  • FAQPage on key landing pages — captures featured snippets in SERPs
  • BreadcrumbList sitewide — clearer SERP path display
  • Product schema for terra HR System SaaS pricing
  • WebSite + sitelinks search box for branded queries

03
Internal Linking Topology
Hub-and-spoke architecture across 4 clusters
  • Pillar pages for each service vertical (payroll, HR, accounting, tax) — designated authority hubs
  • Topical clusters of 8-15 supporting articles per pillar, all linking up to pillar with descriptive anchor text
  • Layer 1 → Layer 2 cross-links: utility content references service pages contextually where intent overlaps (e.g. tax tools → tax services)
  • Crawl depth limit: every commercial page reachable in ≤ 3 clicks from homepage
  • Anchor diversity: balance between exact-match, partial-match, and branded anchors across the site
  • Bilingual EN / VI mirroring via hreflang implementation
04
Crawl & Indexation
Continuous monitoring + GSC ops
  • Dynamic XML sitemap split by content type, submitted + monitored in GSC
  • 301 redirect mapping for legacy URLs after rebuild — zero indexed-traffic loss
  • robots.txt audit — staging blocks, parameter exclusions, crawl-budget allocation
  • Canonical strategy for paginated and filtered content
  • Coverage report monitored weekly — discover, indexed, excluded, valid with warnings
  • MCP-integrated GSC tooling for automated query/page-level audits and quick-win detection
05 - Strategy

How the traffic
actually works

Worth being explicit - because the numbers tell two different stories depending on how you read them. The growth came from a deliberate dual-funnel architecture, not a single optimisation win.

Layer 1 - Volume
High-traffic informational content
Utility pages and HR compliance guides targeting high-volume informational queries - tax lookup tools, payroll calculators, labour law explainers. Top keyword "tra cứu mã số thuế" alone drove 51,000+ clicks. These users aren't buying payroll services today.

Function: Build domain authority at scale, signal topical relevance across the HR/payroll/tax space, establish terra as a trusted resource in the category.
Layer 2 - Intent
B2B service pages optimised for buyers
Service pages targeting payroll outsourcing, HR consulting, accounting, and the terra SaaS platform - separate keyword clusters, commercial copy structure, conversion-focused CTAs. These pages benefit directly from the authority built by Layer 1.

Logic: A domain ranking for 978 keywords and generating 31M total impressions competes significantly better for commercial B2B queries than one with no organic footprint.
Architecture flow
High-volume utility content (Layer 1) earns topical authority that compounds into the domain. Authority lifts B2B service pages (Layer 2) into competitive ranking positions for commercial intent queries — without those pages needing massive direct backlinks.

Utility traffic is not vanity traffic - it's infrastructure. This is a deliberate compounding strategy: Layer 1 builds the domain's authority ceiling, Layer 2 converts that authority into qualified reach for B2B decision-makers.

08 - Reflection

What I learned

The biggest lesson from terra: technical health is not a one-time fix - it's a continuous discipline. The 55→90 health score improvement happened over months of iterative auditing, not a single sprint. The sites that compound are the ones that get incrementally cleaner every week.

The dual-funnel strategy was also a lesson in playing the long game. Early months felt slow - utility pages drove traffic but no obvious commercial signal. Trusting the compounding logic required holding conviction against the instinct to chase short-term conversions. By month 8, the domain authority built by informational content was visibly lifting the service pages in rankings.

Working as the sole technical and creative operator in a small team taught me to ruthlessly prioritise. Not every page needs to be perfect on launch - a published, indexable page with good fundamentals outperforms a perfect page that ships three months late. Ship, measure, iterate.

Finally: AI didn't make me faster at writing - it made me faster at thinking. The real productivity gain wasn't in generating text. It was in using AI to rapidly test angles, surface gaps, and structure thinking before committing to production.
